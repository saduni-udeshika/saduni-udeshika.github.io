Project Scope

Research Gap

In regions prone to frequent rainfall-induced disasters like floods and landslides, current disaster management systems suffer from fragmentation, focusing on different aspects of disaster response. This fragmentation hinders holistic disaster comprehension and decision-making. Moreover, existing methods often neglect multi-hazard interactions, failing to account for complex scenarios. Pre- and post-disaster analyses are also separated, causing data and response planning to be disjointed. To address these critical gaps, our project introduces the 'Knowledge Graph for Multi-Hazard Risk Assessment and Management in Rainfall-Prone Regions.' We leverage Knowledge Graphs and cutting-edge machine learning to create an integrated, efficient, and sophisticated disaster risk assessment approach. By bridging these divides and harnessing advanced technologies, we aim to revolutionize disaster management in rain-prone areas, fostering adaptability and resilience.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

The research problem at the heart of this study revolves around the inadequacies and challenges inherent in contemporary disaster management systems, particularly in regions prone to rainfall-induced disasters like floods and landslides. These critical gaps can be categorized into several key areas. Firstly, there is a pressing issue of fragmented disaster management systems, where different organizations and entities tend to prioritize diverse aspects of disaster response, such as infrastructure, environmental preservation, or humanitarian aid. This fragmentation not only hinders a holistic understanding of disasters but also leads to inefficient resource allocation and coordination. Secondly, there exists a lack of comprehensive risk assessment methodologies. Conventional disaster management often focuses on single-hazard risk assessment, ignoring the intricate interdependencies among multiple types of hazards, especially in scenarios where rainfall triggers a cascade of disaster events. To bridge this gap, it is imperative to develop approaches that consider the complexities of multi-hazard scenarios. Furthermore, the research problem encompasses the challenge of dealing with data complexity and integration. Current disaster management systems struggle to effectively manage and integrate the vast amount of data required for comprehensive risk assessment and decision-making. This complexity impedes timely and informed responses to disasters. Moreover, prediction and preparedness are areas in need of improvement. The current state of disaster management often lacks advanced predictive capabilities and preparedness measures, particularly when it comes to rainfall-related hazards. Developing more accurate prediction models and enhancing preparedness strategies are critical aspects of addressing this gap. Usability and decision support are also areas of concern. Existing disaster management tools and systems may not be user-friendly, and decision-makers may face challenges in accessing and interpreting the available data. An improved usability framework and decision support system are essential for efficient disaster response. Lastly, the research problem involves the absence of holistic disaster analysis. Separating pre- and post-disaster assessments can lead to inefficiencies and missed opportunities for growth and learning. Integrating these analyses into a seamless process is essential to enhance disaster management effectiveness. In summary, this research problem underscores the urgent need to address these gaps in disaster management systems in rainfall-prone regions. By tackling fragmentation, enhancing risk assessment, improving data management, advancing prediction and preparedness, facilitating usability and decision support, and promoting holistic analysis, this study aims to revolutionize the way disasters are comprehended, managed, and mitigated in vulnerable areas.

Solution

RainRisk NET presents a comprehensive solution to address the identified challenges in disaster management within rainfall-prone regions. Our innovative approach harnesses the power of smart technology to significantly enhance disaster management processes. Through the development of the 'Knowledge Graph for Multi-Hazard Risk Assessment and Management,' we aim to provide a holistic and integrated platform that consolidates data, predicts rainfall-induced disasters like floods and landslides, and facilitates rapid decision-making through a user-friendly dashboard. This advanced tool leverages Knowledge Graph technology to contextualize disaster-related data comprehensively, enabling sophisticated analyses and a more profound understanding of multi-hazard scenarios. By seamlessly integrating data sources and employing predictive modeling, RainRisk NET empowers decision-makers with real-time insights into evolving disaster situations, enabling them to make informed and timely decisions. Our user-friendly dashboard ensures that disaster management professionals, as well as local communities, can access critical information easily. This approach not only improves response times but also enhances coordination among different organizations and stakeholders involved in disaster management.

Project Scope

Methodology

proposed system "RainRisk NET Disaster Intelligence" contains 5 parts.

1. Comprehensive Data Collection : The research methodology begins with meticulous data collection from reliable sources such as the Sri Lanka Meteorological Department, National Building Research Organization, Disaster Management Centre, and websites. This diverse dataset forms the foundation for subsequent modeling and research activities.

2. Advanced Machine Learning Models : The research utilizes cutting-edge machine learning models, including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), and the Segformer architecture, implemented using Python and TensorFlow. These models enable accurate image segmentation, time-series analysis, and data processing, significantly enhancing disaster analysis capabilities.

3. Knowledge Graph Integration : The proposed Knowledge Graph serves as a structured representation that links entities, objects, and events related to disaster forecasting, environmental damage assessment, building damage assessment, and humanitarian risk assessment. It is enriched with predictive knowledge derived from machine learning outputs, facilitating complex data contextualization and advanced analytics.

4. Web Application and interactive Dashboard : An interactive web application, developed using ReactJS and MongoDB, provides users with seamless access to disaster prediction, environmental damage assessment, building damage assessment, and humanitarian risk assessment. It offers a user-friendly interface, empowering users to explore various disaster-related topics and visualize data effectively.

5. Exceptional Model Performance The research results demonstrate exceptional model performance across all aspects, including building damage assessment, environmental damage assessment, humanitarian risk assessment, and disaster forecasting. High accuracy rates, precision, recall, and F1-scores underscore the reliability and effectiveness of the integrated approach, providing a solid foundation for disaster management and resilience improvement.
